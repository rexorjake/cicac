CUSTOMS INDUSTRY CONSULTATIVE AND ADVISORY COUNCIL (CICAC)

Partnering for Progress

A New Era of Trade Collaboration

The CICAC is the vital platform institutionalized by the Bureau of Customs (BOC) under CMO No. 02-2024 to foster open dialogue, transparency, and a robust partnership between the government and trade stakeholders. Our mission is to transform customs administration through collective action and mutual accountability.

ABOUT THE COUNCIL

Our Mandate & Structure

The Bridge to Better Customs

The CICAC was established to serve as the consultative body between the BOC and the entire business-industrial sector. We ensure that Customs' reform agenda is responsive to the needs of trade partners and aligned with international best practices.

Core Objectives

Policy & Reform

Provide policy feedback, recommend actions to prevent illicit activities, and identify procedures that are no longer aligned with modern business standards.

Information Exchange

Facilitate regular consultative meetings and dialogues to discuss trade trends, updates, and BOC programs.

Capacity Building

Aid in developing programs to incentivize stakeholder compliance and participate in corruption risk analysis.

Structure

Organizational Structure

The Central CICAC is Chaired by the BOC Commissioner and composed of BOC officials and leading representatives from approved trade associations and compliant companies.

Key Achievements

ATA Carnet System Launch (July 2024)

A major milestone simplifying the temporary admission of goods, developed in collaboration with the Philippine Chamber of Commerce and Industry. This reduces bottlenecks and transaction costs for businesses.

The "Passport for Goods" is officially launched.

Streamlining Cargo Logistics

Continuous proposed amendments to customs regulations are driven by CICAC recommendations, directly leading to improvements in cargo clearance efficiency and trade security.

Updates on ongoing digitalization initiatives at a CICAC meeting.

Recognition

WCO Certificates of Merit

25 key CICAC member organizations were recognized with World Customs Organization Certificates of Merit for their excellence and commitment to facilitating trade and securing borders.
